What are Iris Tumors?

Iris tumors are abnormal growths that develop in the iris, the colored part of the eye. These can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Early detection is crucial for successful treatment and preservation of vision.

Common types include iris melanoma, iris nevus, and iris cysts. Symptoms may include changes in iris color, irregular pupil shape, vision changes, or eye pain. However, many iris tumors are asymptomatic and discovered during routine eye examinations.

How Our AI Works

Our iris tumor detection system utilizes state-of-the-art Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) trained on thousands of iris images. The deep learning model has been carefully optimized to identify subtle patterns and abnormalities that may indicate the presence of tumors.

The AI analyzes multiple features including texture, color distribution, vascular patterns, and structural abnormalities. It provides a confidence score along with its prediction to help medical professionals make informed decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

No. This tool is designed for screening purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider.
How accurate is the AI model?
Our model achieves 98.5% accuracy on validation datasets. However, accuracy can vary depending on image quality and specific conditions. Results should always be verified by medical professionals.
What image formats are supported?
We support all common image formats including JPG, JPEG, PNG, and BMP. For best results, use high-quality, well-lit images of the iris.
